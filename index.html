I created this guide to help digital marketers succeed in a very challenging industry. Existing resources tend to be laden with buzzwords, purely theoretical, and impossible to apply to real-world situations.

Who is this guide for?

This guide is primarily aimed at digital marketing professionals who sit within the marketing teams of B2B investment management firms. However, anyone may find parts of it useful.

How will this guide help you?

This guide will help you create your own digital marketing strategy. We will use a fictional investment management firm to explain concepts and demonstrate how to tackle real-life projects, which you can then adapt for your own use.

Industry-specific marketing challenges

Beyond day-to-day work and projects, there are a lot of factors that make the job more difficult than it needs to be:

  • Difficulty measuring the marketing team's contribution. The sales cycle in investment management B2B is typically non-linear and can be very long, with multiple touchpoints and decision makers along the way. Therefore, it’s essentially impossible to measure marketing’s contribution to flows accurately (despite what many marketing agencies will tell you in their pitches).
  • It’s old-fashioned. Investment management is slow moving, is relationship-led and it can still be an old-school relationship led business, where many salespeople – especially in the institutional channel – prize their clients and prefer to meet in person, with a “hands-off” mentality towards marketing. In some firms, the sales team may not want leads sent to them at all
  • Marketing often has a bad reputation around the business. To be frank, lots of marketing teams can be ineffective and project a bad experience with the sales team, rather than demonstrate how they can help sales do better at scale.
  • High profits. Investment management firms make a lot of money, and so there seems to be a lack of pressure on the marketing team. Sometimes it can feel like you're playing at your job, and that nothing you do will really have any impact. It's important to note that this is changing however, as new disrusptive companies move in and fee pressures bite
