Setting Goals and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

In this post
  • Marketing goals, strategies, objectives, and tactics
    • Goals

Setting a primary goal will give your digital marketing strategy some much-needed purpose and direction. You can revisit your goal whenever you want, especially if you're feeling a bit stuck in the weeds, to keep you focused on the big picture.

As your goal will depend on what your firm is trying to achieve, your first step is to determine the primary goal of your firm. Within investment management, this goal is usually to grow assets under management (AUM) and will specify a growth target and a time period in which to achieve it (to make it SMART):

Firm goal

Grow assets under management by 25% over the next 5 years.

While the wider firm can employ various tactics to try and achieve this desired growth — marketing their investment capabilites, publishing research, launching new products and closing or merging others, hiring new portfolio managers, and even acquiring other businesses — the marketing team has a more narrow focus: to support this growth.

Marketing goals, strategies, objectives, and tactics

Marketing's approach is usually documented in a marketing plan that maps out the team's goals, strategies, objectives and tactics (often abbreviated to GSOT).

  • A goal is a broad primary outcome
  • A strategy is the approach you take to achieve a goal
  • An objective is a measurable step you take to achieve a strategy
  • A tactic is a tool you use in pursuing an objective associated with a strategy

The general steps to create your own GSOTs are as follows:

  1. Set a primary goal for the marketing team
  2. Document the strategies you will employ to achieve the goal
  3. Set one or more objectives within each strategy
  4. Document the tactics you will employ to achieve each objective

Keep in mind that this part of the process is more an art than a science, and can easily grow unmanageable — so don't overcomplicate it. Ultimately, your specific approach will depend on your firm's size, maturity, culture, client base, sales process, and the countries or regions in which it operates. A simplified example can demonstrate what I mean here:

Consider two investment firms, both with AUM of $1 billion:

  • Firm A, a US company, has 10,000 financial advisor clients with an typical account size of $100,000. Sales teams spend a lot of time on the phone, are commision-based, deal with smaller transaction sizes, and are constantly on the lookout for new business opportunities.
  • Firm B, an Italian company, has 70 large institutional clients with a typical account size of $15 million. The sales team meet clients face-to-face, pride themselves on their highly personalised service, and win new business mostly through networking efforts. It can take months or years of working with a potential client before they make an initial investment.

Both firms set the same goal of growing their AUM by 25% within 5 years. Here's how each company could set marketing goals, strategies, objectives and tactics to fit their individual characteristics:

Firm A Firm B
Goal Support 25% growth in firm AUM within 5 years
Strategies Send qualified leads to the sales team to help them win new business Enhance sales team's client servicing & prospecting efforts with information on what their clients and prospects are engaging with digitally
Objectives 500 qualifed leads per year 1,000 activity alerts per year
Tactics
  • Publish blog content to attract website visitors
  • Convert visitors into leads by collecting contact details to download valuable content or subscribe to the blog
  • Qualify leads to make sure they can buy your products
  • Send leads to sales for follow-up when ready
  • Publish content
  • Distribute content to clients & prospects with marketing emails
  • Send real-time alerts to sales team when clients & prospects engage with content

Next, we'll cover each step in more detail.

Goals

Don't lose any sleep over this one. You can simply create your primary marketing goal by slightly re-wording the firm goal:

Goal

Support 25% firm AUM growth over the next 5 years.

Strategies

While the choice of available marketing strategies can seem endless, I actually believe there only two options available to B2B investment management marketing teams — both of which fit neatly within our inbound marketing framework:

Strategy 1

Win new business from previously unknown clients by sending qualified leads to the sales team for follow-up.

Strategy 2

Retain assets and win additional business from existing clients by sending information on client activity to the sales team for follow-up.

The first strategy covers the the attract, convert, engage and qualify steps from the inbound marketing framework; the second spans engage and inform.

Once you've decided on which strategies you will use, every future piece of work within the digital marketing should fall within one of your chosen strategies — posting blogs, running events, brand marketing, product launches, and so on.

Objectives

Objectives are like mini-goals for your strategies. We can set as many as we want.

Because there are only two possible strategies, that helps simplify this too.

The objectives are the tangible things that you want to achieve. These could be to generate a certain number of leads, grow brand awareness by a specific percentage, gain a specific amount of search traffic. Objectives are the clear and measurable things that you want to achieve from your digital marketing execution. You may have multiple objectives that you use to achieve your strategy.

Tactics

Our tactics are the day-to-day things we will do to achieve our objectives. While the full list of possible options can seem long and complicated, it's actually much more simple:

  1. Create content & experiences — blog posts, videos, landing pages, PDFs, webinars, and in-person events
  2. Actively promote content — through posting content to company social media accounts, subscription emails, event invites, and various types of advertising
  3. Behind the scenes stuff — this is all the internal-only tactics, which are lead generation, lead grading, lead scoring, and activity alerts

Here we must differentiate active promototion from passive — search engine and referral traffic.
